Chicken Biryani Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • Water for cooking rice
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (for garnish)
  • Fried onions (for garnish)
  • Ghee (clarified butter) for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Marinating the Chicken:

    2. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Cooking Biryani Rice:

    3. Wash the Basmati rice thoroughly and soak it for 30 minutes.

    In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the soaked and drained rice, green cardamom, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, and salt. Cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked (it should still have a bite). Drain and set aside.

  3. Preparing Biryani Masala:

    4. In a large, heavy-bottomed pan, heat the oil or ghee.

    Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.

    Add the sliced onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.

    Add the green chilies, chopped tomatoes, and cook until the tomatoes are soft and oil starts to separate.

    Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes.

  4. Layering and Dum Cooking:

    5. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of cooked rice.

    Add a layer of the marinated chicken.

    Sprinkle chopped mint and cilantro over the chicken.

    Repeat the layers until all the rice and chicken are used up.

    Drizzle saffron milk over the top, garnish with fried onions, and drizzle ghee.

    Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal the edges with dough.

    Cook on low heat (dum cooking) for 20-25 minutes until the chicken is cooked, and the flavors meld.

    Once done, fluff the biryani gently with a fork and serve hot.

Chicken Biryani is best enjoyed with raita or a side salad. Enjoy your homemade Chicken Biryani!
