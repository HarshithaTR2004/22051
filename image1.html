Poori Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

    Prepare the Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour, salt, sugar, and 1 tablespoon of oil.
  2. Gradually add water while kneading to form a smooth, firm, and non-sticky dough. The dough should be slightly stiff.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
    4. Divide the Dough:

  4. After resting, divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls. You can make around 10-12 balls from this dough.
    5. Roll the Dough:

  5. Take one dough ball and roll it between your palms to make a smooth ball.
  6. Flatten the ball slightly.
    7. Roll Out the Poori:

  7. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadai on medium heat.
  8. Take a dough ball and roll it out into a thin, circular disc using a rolling pin. It should be about 4-5 inches in diameter. Make sure the edges are thinner than the center; this helps the poori puff up while frying.
    9. Fry the Poori:

  9. To check if the oil is hot enough for frying, drop a small piece of dough into the oil. If it sizzles and rises to the top, the oil is ready.
  10. Gently slide the rolled poori into the hot oil. It should puff up within a few seconds.
  11. Use a slotted spoon to gently press the poori down, helping it puff up evenly.
  12. Fry the poori until it turns golden brown, then flip it to the other side and fry until both sides are evenly cooked.
    13. Remove and Drain:

  13. Once the poori is golden brown and crispy, remove it from the oil using a slotted spoon.
  14. Place it on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
    15. Repeat:

  15. Follow the same process for the remaining dough balls.
    16. Serve hot pooris with your favorite side dishes such as potato masala, chana masala, or any other curry of your choice.
