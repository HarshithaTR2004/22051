Samosa Recipe

Ingredients:

    1. For the Samosa Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for kneading
    • 2.For the Samosa Filling:

  • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

    For the Samosa Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, carom seeds, and salt.
  2. Add the oil or ghee and rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a firm and smooth dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and set it aside for 30 minutes.
    4. For the Samosa Filling:

  4. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat.
  5. Add the cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and fennel seeds. Allow them to sizzle.
  6. Add grated ginger and chopped green chilies. Saut√© for a minute.
  7. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes, green peas, and all the dry spices (turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala). Stir well and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  8. Add salt to taste and mix in the chopped cilantro. Remove the filling from heat and let it cool.
    9. Assembling the Samosas:

  9. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls and roll each ball into a thin oval or round shape.
  10. Cut the rolled dough in half to form two semi-circles.
  11. Create a cone shape by folding the semi-circle and sealing the edges with water. You'll have a triangular pocket.
  12. Fill each pocket with the prepared potato filling, then fold and seal the open edge to form a triangle-shaped samosa.
    13. Frying the Samosas:

  13. Heat oil in a deep pan for deep frying over medium-high heat.
  14. Gently slide the samosas into the hot oil. Fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  15. Remove the fried samosas and drain excess oil on paper towels.

    16. Serve your homemade samosas hot with tamarind chutney or mint chutney. Enjoy!
